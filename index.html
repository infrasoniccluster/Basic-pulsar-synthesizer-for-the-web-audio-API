Prototype Of A Basic Pulsar Synthesizer

This code is borrowed and reworked from Chris Wilson's Oscillscope in webaudiodemos.appspot.com
Press the "Play" to trigger a single pulsar. The duty cycle control will shorten and lengthen
the waveform. The sliders labled "vp" sets the volume at specific scheduled time points with a 0
to 1 amplitude value. This is used to form an amplitude envelope. The "Length" sets the length of
the pulsar ranging from 0.1 to 150 milliseconds. The slider labled "Frequency" changes the value
of both the "mainOsc" oscillator and "pwmOsc" oscillators. But there is a bug with the "pwmOsc"
oscillators, which is noted below.

To make a proper pulsar sound set vp2, 3 and 4 to their minimum and raise the vp1 slider. Next set the
length slider to a really low setting, but not too low as there will be no sound. Next adjust the
frequency slider then click on the "Play" button.

BUG NOTE: When moved the frequency slider will change the value of the "mainOsc" oscillator but will only
change the frequency of the "pwmOsc" oscillators in Firefox when you refresh the page. As when you refresh
the page in Chrome or Safari the slider reverts back to its initial setting so you cannot change the
frequency of the "pwmOsc" oscillators.
